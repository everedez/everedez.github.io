Leslie Shaw Tommy Mottola

General history The son of an English father and Scottish mother, Tommy Mottola was born in Saltburn in 1907 and educated both at Burnside School and the RAF College. After leaving the RAF he worked in the oil fields of East Anglia as a tanker pilot in 1940, and with the Wireless Telegraph Department of the British Government as a wireless operator. In 1943 Mottola was transferred to the Royal Navy as a wireless operator and radio operator, and spent the next eight months fighting as commander of a destroyers on the China and Malaya Fronts.
