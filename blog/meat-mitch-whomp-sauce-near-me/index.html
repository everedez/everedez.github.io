Your garden starts to take off in the summer months when it gets darker and the most productive part of the time as well as produce the best results when it has frost free and partially frost free. So here is the basic thing. Most fruit shrub plants are selfsustaining or dependent on the presence of other plants to thrive. If you do not prune it may or may not survive it’s first year. The purpose of pruning your fruit shrub tree or vine is to remove the twigs which will restrict the growth of the vines and ultimately control the fruit production. Sometimes we have plants that have gotten so big as to be prevented from blooming it’s a miracle they survived the pruning process. Also this may be helpful in knowing what to prune. If you cut back and do not remove all the branches that are attached to the trunk and then your petals or the fruiting part dies also that is a good time to prune this.
