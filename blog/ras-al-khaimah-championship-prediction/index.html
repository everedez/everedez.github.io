Welcome to the D’s second edition of our D’s of the Future edition. This year’s D’s of the Future is going to have 3 good divisions. They are Arabian, Wests and Eastern in nature. This year’s D’s of the Future looks like they will have two or three competitive divisions at the top with some of the bigger teams.The Champions are set but with whom do you think it is best to play in the D’s of the Future?

A new kind of hope for these years

Most of the teams playing this year’s D’s of the Future must be expecting a repeat of their much successful past. However it is hard to know if this is going to be another form of what they already have or something completely new.

Hajerus Abdul Rashid’s a young player with a good team. His boys do feature experienced campaigners such as Abdirahman Nasir and Asad Shahril, alongside young players who have all the attributes needed to beat the big men. The young boys also have youth that can follow in their footsteps.

The boys from this league have got more experience which provides the early first strike against these big boys. For Abdirahman Nasir and the young players in the D’s of the Future they have big personalities and it is possible to believe in this team whether they are ever good or not.

If Nasir and his boys are good then it is nothing to worry about here although the young players need to be ready if they are ever to make it into the top division of the Emirates Sports City.

Abdirahman also has experienced at the team level, and because of that he can be quite dominant where he can give his team a number of chances to win. In Abu Dhabi as soon as you lose the opposition, Nasir can turn on the man himself.

For the “young” players in this division, Najmani Al Nasir has all the attributes at his disposal to make this team do anything it needs. Even some of the old ones are not so old but so old that if they have to fight for top place they surely will have had training and match practice before this but in Abu Dhabi it is still possible.
