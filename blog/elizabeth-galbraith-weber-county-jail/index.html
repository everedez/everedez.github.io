Official records of the County of Weber, Utah
Weber County Records, 1865-1980

The Weber County records are the basis for many Utah historical research projects and, in many instances, are the only independent source for information about an area. The Historical Division of the Weber County Clerk&#x27;s office records comprise both county records and private records. They are collected, edited, and researched by volunteers and may be accessed on request. Utah State Archivists maintain a large collection of historical artifacts, documents, photographs, memorabilia, and other items that are examples of public and private documents and materials. A full, unabridged text of the historical records is available through the historic database at WeberCountyHistory.org. The archive is also available as an Internet archive.

The Dorn Children House Museum, Weber

There is a full-scale recreation area with the Dorn Children House Museum on display in the main building of the Dorn Family House in the Main Street West Historic District of Weber County, Utah. The museum contains local treasures, drawings, and photographs from the period prior to 1900. Find a variety of exhibits on the History of the Dorn children that will suit your interest.

The Weber County Historical Library

The Weber County Historical Library holds several collections of historical items, as well as genealogical information. The Library was founded in 1893 and is housed on the second floor of the Historic Downtown Main Building at the corner of Weber and South Streets in Weber, Utah. The library has the largest collection of county historical documents in the nation. There is no charge for viewing these materials. The Library is open 365 days a year, Monday through Saturday.
