About Kudus

Kudus is an American hip hop group and one-half of the production team Kudus who was founded in Los Angeles, California by Dr. Jada Pinkett Smith, a renowned recording artist, and Derek Dessner, a Grammy award-winner. The two met at a birthday party for Smith&#x27;s daughter Hannah which Dessner also attended on a school trip to Los Angeles. At the time, Dessner and Smith had been recording as a duo under the name, DJ Muggs. By the time the duo had independently released their first album, Dr. Jada Pinkett Smith &amp; The Kudus, the name Kudus was coined. The album brought the duo nationwide attention and gained both the producer and the artist attention as the duo won Best New Artist in 2007 at the APRA Awards. The duo have continued to release an album a year since then, although this is more sporadic than the previous album. Among their earliest hits, the duo have created hit songs for Sean Paul, Jazmine Sullivan, Frank Ocean and more than a dozen others.
