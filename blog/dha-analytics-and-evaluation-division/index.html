DHA Analytics &amp; Evaluation Division

The Dha Analytics &amp; Evaluation Division (DHA) is responsible for making the decision to buy, and for overseeing a significant share of the sale of the resources of India’s leading energy company, Bharat Petroleum Corporation Ltd (BPCL). The Dha Analytics &amp; Evaluation Division is run by an office at Bengaluru.

Responsibilities:

·Create, monitor and evaluate projects

·Assess how the BPCL environment is performing in Dha Division

·Create and monitor trade reports such as Indian Power Market Report, Indian Power Spot Report and Emission Credit Tracker Report

·Create and evaluate Annual Reports for BPCL Corporate Finance, Corporate Reports and Internal Reports
·Contribute to the Indian Power Market Report
·Contribute to the Indian Power Spot Report and Emission Credit Tracker Report
·Re-evaluate whether further outsourcing of India’s power sector activities is a viable option
·Monitor and lead on BPCL Environmental Management Programs and their associated activities

·Monitor work being done by BPCL Environmental Department, Indian Water Commission, Directorate of Power and Energy, National Powerhouse, Government of India, Environmental Education Institutions/Institutions etc.

·Coordinate BPCL information technology efforts with the Foreign Offices and Foreign Investment Departments of relevant countries.

·Monitor BPCL activities to ensure regulatory compliance and compliance with Indian Government’s export control laws and regulations

·Contribute to the Quality Management System for Bharat Petroleum Corporation Ltd
·Contribute to the Government of India’s (GOI)/ statutory authority’s Climate Change Policy and National Green Policy
·Monitor and review trading activities for the India Power Market Report
·Supervise employees with respect to energy-related functions and responsibilities
·Receive and review technical requests/warnings
·Review India’s energy program
·Adequate knowledge of India’s energy program to ensure that changes are made without compromising policy objectives

·Contribute to India’s global Energy Resource Efficiency Leadership programme (ERELP) that is overseen by International Energy Agency

•Requirements:

The position is currently performing the following responsibilities.

·To understand, monitor and evaluate the energy environment in India.
