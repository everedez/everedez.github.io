Kyrgyzstan under the helm of dictator
ADIKER - People’s Choice is the only website that covers life under the dictatorship of
Kyrgyzstan.
The Military Commissariat for Internal Security in
Kyrgyzstan supervises the country’s military branches. The website has documented

the suppression of dissidents, which we described in an earlier article:

http://www.people-
choice.org/index.php?act=story&amp;cid=11
The current president of the republic, a civilian, Khazakstan
Minister of Internal Affairs Nazaray Mashiyev, gave a press briefing on Monday to
support Khazakstan’s tough measures in the fight against terrorism. But the
ambiguities are clear. His speech focused on Ukraine, and he mentioned nothing
about human rights violations. Then, a reporter asked him how Khazakstan planned
to keep their promise to release 31 detainees from the war prison. He laughed and
claimed they had already changed the prisoners’ terms. The human rights group
Kyrgyzstan Working Group later published a note on its website
informing
the public they have transferred another
32 individuals from
Kyrgyzstan Prison 1 to Khazakstan’s
Amnesty International Project.
It is not yet clear whether these were released because it’s necessary or
whether they were freed under false pretenses. The website mentioned in our
article is an example of the Khazakstan war on dissent. It is not restricted to
relating to anti-extremism, human rights or war crimes.
