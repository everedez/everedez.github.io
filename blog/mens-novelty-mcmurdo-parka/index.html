The Old Globe Theatre (NY)

Enriched with more than 250 years of experience in stage productions, Edward Downey’s work explores the rich world of theatrical lighting and show business. He has penned the plays “The Old Globe Treadmill,” “Greenback Gingham” and “A True Whistle.” He’s also staged Shakespeare and classics ranging from “Midsummer Night’s Dream” to ”The History Man” and “Alice in Wonderland.” Read More » “Listed in the National Register of Historic Places. He holds bachelor’s and master’s degrees from Penn State.”

Charles Kemble Playhouse (Vermont)

A true favorite, Charles Kemble’s magnificent stage productions and musical numbers are a blend of wit, charm and deadpan humor. His productions run the gamut from vaudeville gags to dramatic interludes. Themes of musical comedy, cross-dressing, lust and alienation, character study — yes, all here. The Charles Kemble Company’s annual production of Jitterbug Performs: A Comedy of Musical Theater is scheduled for Wednesday at the Charles Kemble Playhouse!
